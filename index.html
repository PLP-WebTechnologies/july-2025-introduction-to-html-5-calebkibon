Types of Bridges in Civil Engineering

  • Beam Bridge
  • Truss Bridge
  • Arch Bridge
  • Suspension Bridge
  • Cable-Stayed Bridge
  • Cantilever Bridge

Beam Bridge

A beam bridge is the simplest type of bridge, consisting of a horizontal beam supported at each end. It is ideal for short spans and is cost-effective to build.

Truss Bridge

Truss bridges use a triangular framework to distribute loads efficiently. They are commonly used for medium to long spans due to their high strength-to-weight ratio.

Arch Bridge

Arch bridges rely on a curved structure that transfers loads into horizontal thrusts at the supports. They are suitable for locations with solid foundation conditions.

Suspension Bridge

Suspension bridges use cables suspended between towers to support the deck. They are used for very long spans and are known for their flexibility and elegance.

Cable-Stayed Bridge

Cable-stayed bridges feature cables connected directly from the tower to the bridge deck. They are structurally efficient and used for medium to long spans.

Cantilever Bridge

Cantilever bridges are built using projecting beams anchored only at one end. These structures allow construction over obstacles without the need for falsework.
